Designers

Alexander Rodchenko 1891-1955 (an important figure in the Russian forefront, perhaps not so much for his pictorial "work" as for his teaching, his personality as an animator and polygrapher. After being influenced by Malevich, he found himself on Tatlin's side in their eternal quarrels, before becoming one of the leaders of the "productivist" movement and (temporarily) abandoning painting).

El Lissitzky 1890-1941 (architect, painter, photographer, typographer. An architect who never built, he was nevertheless one of the catalysts of international Constructivism in the 1920s. In addition to his major contributions to the history of graphic design and his museographic innovations, he was one of the main theorists of a new synthesis of the arts which, based on the notion of environment, sought to redefine the place of the spectator).
