Typography

Grasset (In Paris, painter and decorator Eugène Grasset (1845-1917) designed numerous ornaments and vignettes in this spirit, as well as an alphabet, the Grasset, published in 1897 by the Peignot foundry).

Designers

Alfons Mucha 1860-1939 (The work of Czech painter and designer Alfons Mucha embodies the Belle Époque and its Art Nouveau style. Famous for his posters for shows, exhibitions and consumer products, the artist was also a designer of ornaments and typefaces, attentive to the revival of books, and a decorator of jewelry, stained glass, furniture, tableware, exhibition stands and stores).

Peter Behrens 1868-1940 (In 1890, he moved to Munich, where he began his career as a painter, illustrator and bookbinder, before returning to Düsseldorf to teach at the School of Applied Arts).

Works
